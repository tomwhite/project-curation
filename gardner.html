Martin Gardner Projects

Many of the projects I've worked on have been inspired by Martin Gardner's work. Here are some of my projects from the Canon.

1. The Scientific American Book of Mathematical Puzzles and Diversions (1959)

Polyominoes. I wrote a program to explore polyominoes (and other tiling patterns) called Alhambra

2. The 2nd Scientific American Book of Mathematical Puzzles & Diversions (1961)

Recreational Topology. The Addendum mentions Shannon's Bird Cage game, which I recreated in Arduino form.

4. The Unexpected Hanging and Other Mathematical Diversions (1969)

The Transcendental Number e. Inspired by a note in the Addendum, I created an animation of Euler's equation, which I called The Most Beautiful Formula.

6. Mathematical Carnival (1975)

Piet Hein's Superellipse. I've written a few Superellipse generators, here's my latest.

11. Knotted Doughnuts and Other Mathematical Entertainments (1986)

Look-See Proofs. I created some postcards with an image proving that the sum of the first n cubes equals the square of the sum of the first n numbers.

13. Penrose Tiles and Trapdoor Ciphers...and the Return of Dr. Matrix (1988)

Penrose Tiling. I wrote my Alhambra program (see above) originally to explore and produce images of Penrose tilings.

Mandelbrot's Fractals. I created some L-Systems to draw fractal curves from this chapter. I've also re-created a Mandelbrot program I wrote in the 80s.

15. The Last Recreations: Hydras, Eggs, and Other Mathematical Mystifications (1997)

The Symmetry Creations of Scott Kim. This led me to Scott Kim's "Inversions" book, which inspired me to make two designs for my daughters
